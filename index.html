About me

Welcome to my academic profile. My name is Thomas Shaw, currently serving as the 2020 Bill Gole Fellow at the School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, University of Queensland. My research focuses on exploring the heterogeneity of MND subtypes through advanced imaging techniques (MRI).

Academic Background

  • 2015-2016: Master of Neuroscience (Advanced), Australian National University
  • 2010-2014: Bachelor of Psychological Science (Honours), The University of Queensland

  • 2017-2019: Research Assistant, Queensland Brain Institute, UQ

Career Summary

I completed my PhD in Neuroscience and Medical Imaging at The University of Queensland in 2020, where I used multi-contrast MRI to characterise brain tissue in MND using sophisticated image-processing techniques. During this time, I was responsible for scanning and analysing data for over 300 participants and I was trained as an MR operator. My research has included MR methods for determining the myelin content of brain regions, robust and reproducible image processing algorithms for characterising MRI data, and data processing pipelines for the analysis of heterogeneous brain morphometry.

I have used computational techniques in several imaging modalities including structural and quantitative MR, DWI fMRI, and spectroscopy.

I was awarded the prestigious Bill Gole Postdoctoral Fellowship for Motor Neuron Research Australia in April 2021. In this 3-year fellowship, I explored the heterogeneity of MND subtypes by first developing and implementing spinal cord imaging techniques at UQ and combining these with deep clinical and neuropsychological phenotyping.
